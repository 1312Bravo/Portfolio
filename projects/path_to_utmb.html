Path To UTMB

Home

The UTMB (Ultra-Trail du Mont-Blanc), often called the Olympics of trail running, brings together the world’s best athletes in Chamonix each August. Chamonix becomes the centre of the sport as runners gather to race around the Mont Blanc massif, crossing the mountains of France, Italy, and Switzerland. Alongside the elites, thousands of amateurs chase the same dream.

The UTMB Finals is a race week featuring multiple distances and profiles. The main race, UTMB itself, is about 170 km with over 10,000 meters of elevation gain. Other iconic races include CCC (~100 km) and OCC (~50 km), each linking key alpine towns and attracting different profiles of runners.

The Finals are the culmination of the UTMB World Series, where runners qualify by collecting “Running Stones” at races worldwide or earning direct qualification through top performances. For many, it is a multi-year journey just to reach the start line.

As a trail runner myself, UTMB sits in the category of long-term dreams that shape how I train. From an analytical perspective, it’s also a fascinating system to study, helping me better understand both my preparation and what it takes to one day race in Chamonix.

Scrape UTMB Race and Runners statistics - Data

Behind every UTMB analysis lies one major challenge: getting the data. A lot of valuable UTMB data exists publicly, but rarely in a format that is easy to analyze. To make large-scale analysis possible, I built a collection of Python functions that scrape and organize race results, runner statistics, and historical performance data directly from UTMB webpages. The pipeline supports multiple races and runner profiles with a simple, reusable interface — turning scattered web data into analysis-ready datasets. May 2026
